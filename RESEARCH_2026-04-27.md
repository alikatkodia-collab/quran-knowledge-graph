# Quran Knowledge Graph: Research-Backed Critique and Extension Roadmap

*Compiled 2026-04-27 by deep research agent. Sources at the bottom. Skeptical-mode output — the brief was "challenge the stack, don't affirm it."*

## 1. TL;DR — What You're Probably Missing

- **Your embedding model is the single biggest performance bottleneck.** `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` is a 2021-vintage 384-dim English-only model with a 256-token effective context. Replacing it with **BGE-M3** or **Snowflake Arctic Embed 2.0** instantly buys you (a) Arabic embeddings without a separate pipeline, (b) ~8 points of MTEB, and (c) the ability to embed Hafs Arabic *and* Khalifa English in the same space. You currently can't do cross-lingual semantic retrieval — and Quran research is fundamentally cross-lingual.
- **There is a thriving Quran-RAG academic community you don't appear to be tracking.** The IslamicEval 2025 / QIAS 2025 shared tasks at ArabicNLP, the QurSim and QRCD benchmarks, and Tarteel's QUL (Quranic Universal Library) all exist and overlap directly with what you're building. You should be running your QIS against QRCD-eval before claiming anything.
- **Your hallucination stack (cross-encoder NLI + semantic-entropy probes) is good but missing the strongest 2024 result for your specific use case: MiniCheck-FT5** (Tang et al., EMNLP 2024). It is GPT-4-class faithfulness checking at 400× lower cost and is purpose-built for grounding-document fact-checking — exactly verse-citation grounding.
- **Sefaria has effectively shipped the project you're building, just for Jewish texts.** Their Linker, ref-resolver, Topic ontology, and ref-topic-link graph are the blueprint. They open-source the data dump *and* the named-entity model (`Sefaria/he_ref_ner` on HuggingFace). Studying their schema is probably the highest-ROI day you can spend on this project.
- **Your typed-edge taxonomy (SUPPORTS/ELABORATES/QUALIFIES/CONTRASTS/REPEATS) is hand-authored when it could be tafsir-grounded.** The Doha Historical Dictionary RAG paper and the Altafsir.com corpus (110+ tafsir works) give you a ground-truth source for inter-verse relationships. You're inventing a relation taxonomy when classical Islamic scholars already produced one.

## 3. Things That Should Genuinely Challenge Your Current Stack

**3a. `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` is the wrong choice now.**
This is the strongest claim in this report. Your model is monolingual English, 384-dim, 256-token effective. You have Arabic verses sitting unembedded — that's not an oversight, that's a structural limitation of the model you picked. The HN/community consensus from 2025 is explicit: ["Don't use all-MiniLM-L6-v2 for new vector embeddings datasets"](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46081800). Move to BGE-M3 or Arctic Embed L v2.0. You will spend a day re-embedding 6,234 verses (trivial) and gain Arabic+English in one space, plus 8+ MTEB points.

**3b. Your NLI verifier is a 2021-architecture choice you should reconsider — but don't kill it, supplement it.** `cross-encoder/nli-deberta-v3-xsmall` was great in 2022. **MiniCheck-FT5** ([Tang et al., EMNLP 2024](https://aclanthology.org/2024.emnlp-main.499/)) was purpose-built for the exact task you're doing — claim-vs-grounding-document factuality — and beats AlignScore by ~4.3% across LLM-AggreFact. Run them in ensemble; trust both or neither. Honest caveat: NLI for verse citation is *fine* if your verses are short (they are) and English (they are). Don't over-engineer.

**3c. Your typed-edge taxonomy is a guess.** SUPPORTS / ELABORATES / QUALIFIES / CONTRASTS / REPEATS came from "GBrain insight." Classical Islamic scholarship has 1,400 years of tafsir-driven verse-relation taxonomy: *takhsis* (specification), *bayan* (clarification), *naskh* (abrogation — controversial in the Khalifa lens, important to handle deliberately), *takid* (emphasis/repetition), *muhkam/mutashabih*. Mining altafsir.com's 110+ tafsirs with an LLM and aligning to this richer taxonomy would replace a 5-relation hand-spec with a scholarly-grounded 15–25 relation set. The [Quranic Universal Library](https://qul.tarteel.ai/) and [tafsir_api](https://github.com/spa5k/tafsir_api) make this tractable.

**3d. 51K TF-IDF RELATED_TO edges may be doing less work than you think.** TF-IDF shared-keyword over 6,234 verses with a cap of 12 will cluster *vocabulary*, not *meaning*. The 93.7% cross-surah figure is consistent with both "great cross-cutting semantic graph" and "lexical co-occurrence of stop-word-adjacent vocabulary." Add an ablation: build the same edge set with BGE-M3 cosine and compare retrieval-as-traversal recall on QRCD. If TF-IDF doesn't substantially beat dense, kill it.

**3e. Your reasoning-memory graph is interesting and underexploited.** The (Query)-[:TRIGGERED]->(ReasoningTrace)-[:HAS_STEP]->(ToolCall) subgraph is structurally the same thing **HippoRAG** uses for cheap multi-hop retrieval via Personalized PageRank. You've built the substrate; you haven't shipped the algorithm on top. This is probably your highest-ROI single feature.

**3f. Your eval is blind to two failure modes.** QIS = 0.35·citation_recall + 0.25·citation_precision + 0.30·grounding + 0.10·relevance has no notion of (i) **answer abstention quality** — does the system know when to say "the Quran does not address this"? — and (ii) **theological/translator-stance fidelity** — does the answer respect Khalifa's submitter framing or silently smuggle in mainstream Sunni/Shia interpretive priors from the LLM's training data? Add Ragas's `answer_correctness` plus a custom `stance_consistency` probe (cosine to a reference Khalifa-corpus centroid). [Ragas docs](https://docs.ragas.io/en/stable/concepts/metrics/available_metrics/).

**3g. Things that are actually fine and should NOT change.**
- Neo4j as the substrate. With its GenAI plugin, vector indexes, and Cypher, it's the right call for typed-edge + dense.
- Anthropic-only orchestration with Ollama/OpenRouter fallback. Don't churn here. Write better prompts instead.
- The cache-with-dedupe-at-0.98 pattern. Solid.
- The Three.js/Fibonacci-sphere viz. Don't yak-shave it.

## 4. Adjacent Projects to Study (Sefaria First)

**Sefaria** ([sefaria/Sefaria-Project](https://github.com/Sefaria/Sefaria-Project), [developers.sefaria.org](https://developers.sefaria.org/)) is the closest analog to QKG that exists. Specific patterns to steal:

- **The Linker** — automatic citation resolution. ~150 sites use it. Their NER model is open: [`Sefaria/he_ref_ner`](https://huggingface.co/Sefaria/he_ref_ner). For QKG: build an Arabic + English Quranic-citation NER and ship it as a JS widget. Sites quoting ayahs would auto-link to your KG.
- **Topic ontology + ref-topic-links** ([API](https://developers.sefaria.org/reference/get-ref-topic-links)) — separates *topics* (concepts) from *refs* (verse pointers) and links them with provenance and weight. Your Keyword/SemanticDomain layer should look like this. They use Linear Integer Programming for topic-page source selection — applicable to your verse-recommendation queries.
- **Source Sheets** — user-curated multi-text compositions with shared refs. The QKG analog is "submitter study sheets": a curated walk through verses with commentary. This is product, not research, but it's the *use case* the graph enables.
- **Sefaria-Export** — they ship the entire structured dataset under CC. Doing this for your Khalifa-aligned Hafs+keyword+root corpus would attract collaborators.

**Cognee** ([topoteretes/cognee](https://github.com/topoteretes/cognee)) — not a content analog but an architectural one. Their "Cognify → Memify" loop (extract → embed → graph → prune stale → strengthen frequent) is exactly what your reasoning-trace graph wants to become. They benchmark against Mem0, Graphiti, LightRAG on multi-hop QA.

**Logos Bible Software** (proprietary) — their "Factbook," "Sense" search, and inline citation graph have decades of refinement on the closest cousin problem. Not open-source, but their UX patterns are public.

## 5. Recent 2024–2026 Papers Worth Reading

1. **Farquhar et al., "Detecting hallucinations in large language models using semantic entropy"** — [Nature 630, 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07421-0). You already use this conceptually with Haiku probes; the paper proves it works task-agnostically.
2. **Kossen et al., "Semantic Entropy Probes"** — [arXiv:2406.15927](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.15927). Cheap single-pass version of #1. Replaces 5 Haiku probes with one hidden-state probe. Direct cost win.
3. **Tang et al., "MiniCheck"** — [EMNLP 2024](https://aclanthology.org/2024.emnlp-main.499/). GPT-4-class fact-checking at 400× cheaper; the right choice over your current NLI for grounding.
4. **Investigating Retrieval-Augmented Generation in Quranic Studies** — [arXiv:2503.16581](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.16581). 13-LLM Quran-RAG benchmark. Tells you what model size you actually need.
5. **Transformer Tafsir at QIAS 2025** — [arXiv:2509.23793](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.23793v1). Hybrid BM25+dense+cross-encoder pipeline gets 80% on Subtask 2 with Fanar LLM. Direct architectural template.
6. **Optimized Quran Passage Retrieval (Mostafa et al.)** — [arXiv:2412.11431](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.11431). AraBERT-base hits MAP@10 0.36 on expanded QRCD. This is the bar your retrieval should beat.
7. **Doha Historical Dictionary RAG** — [arXiv:2603.23972](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23972). 85%+ accuracy on Quran/Hadith with diachronic lexicographic grounding. Etymology project gold.
8. **MS GraphRAG / LazyGraphRAG / LightRAG / HippoRAG comparison** — [arXiv:2506.05690](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.05690v3). The honest "when does graph RAG actually help" paper.
9. **BGE M3-Embedding** — [arXiv:2402.03216](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.03216). The multilingual dense+sparse+colbert paper.
10. **AlignAR** — [arXiv:2512.21842](https://arxiv.org/html/2512.21842). LLM-based Arabic-English sentence alignment, F1 85.5%. Useful for multi-translation infrastructure.

## Wild Card: Code-19 as Structured Features

Your project is uniquely positioned to do something **no other Quran RAG can do**: take Khalifa's Code-19 mathematical-structure claims as *queryable structured features* in the graph. Frequencies of the letters Q, N, S; gematria of opening Mysterious Letters; verse-counts modulo 19. Encode as `Verse.code19_features` and `Sura.code19_features` properties; let the LLM cite them when relevant, and you have a feature **that cannot be hallucinated** (it's arithmetic over an immutable text) and that aligns with the Submitter framing the Khalifa translation requires. No mainstream Quran-NLP project will touch this; that's exactly why it's defensible territory for QKG.
