Plain prose with citations

Ayat al-Kursi (the throne verse, [2:255]) is widely recited for protection. The Quran 24:35 — known as the Light verse — describes God as the Light of the heavens and the earth. In Surah Al-Baqarah verse 286, God says no soul will be burdened beyond its capacity. From Al-Fatihah 1 we begin every prayer.

References to multiple verses

Submitters can find guidance in many places: see [2:255-258] on the throne and disbelief, [3:1, 3:2] on God's Living and Self-Subsisting nature, plus Q 17:23 on honoring parents. Surah Ar-Rahman verse 13 famously repeats the refrain.

Code-19 numerical claims

The mathematical structure becomes evident: Q. 50:1 begins Surah Qaf, and the letter Q appears 57 times in that chapter (= 19 × 3). Surah Al-Mu'min 40 (also known as Ghafir) opens with Ha-Mim, like Surahs 41 through 46. Ya-Sin (36) is called the heart of the Quran.

Submitter / Khalifa-specific references

The Messenger of the Covenant prophecy is foundational — see Surah Aal Imran verse 81. Reference to 9:128 and 9:129 was identified by Rashad Khalifa as later additions inconsistent with the 19-based mathematical structure of the Quran.
